Introduction

Europe is a continent known for its rich history, diverse cultures, famous landmarks, and well-developed transport infrastructure. Within a relatively small area, it offers high mountains, sea coasts, historic towns, and modern capitals.

Many travellers choose Europe because it makes it possible to discover different languages, cuisines, and traditions within a short time. In many parts of the EU, travelling between countries is also easier and more comfortable.

As Lonely Planet Europe suggests, one of the greatest advantages of travelling in Europe is its diversity. Each region offers a different character, a different atmosphere, and a different way of exploring the world.

"Travelling through Europe is not only a movement between cities, but also an encounter with history, architecture, and everyday life."

Popular Countries in Europe

Italy

A favourite destination for lovers of history, art, the sea, and gastronomy.

Italy is known for cities such as Rome, Venice, Florence, and Milan. Visitors are attracted by ancient monuments, Renaissance art, and the typical Mediterranean atmosphere.

France

A country of fashion, art, museums, and romantic places.

France offers not only Paris and the Eiffel Tower, but also the French Riviera, the castles of the Loire Valley, and a rich culinary tradition. It is suitable for both city tourism and relaxing holidays.

Spain

A sunny country with a colourful culture and famous beaches.

Spain combines modern cities with historical heritage. Tourists often visit Barcelona, Madrid, Seville, and coastal regions. Local culture is connected with music, dance, and festivals.

Austria

An excellent choice for admirers of culture, music, and Alpine nature.

Austria is known for the elegant architecture of Vienna, reliable transport, and mountain regions. In winter it is popular with skiers, while in summer it attracts hikers and lovers of lakes.

Most Beautiful Cities in Europe

  1. Paris – a city of art, galleries, boulevards, and iconic monuments.
  2. Rome – a place where antiquity, Christian tradition, and modern life meet.
  3. Vienna – an elegant capital with a rich musical and cultural tradition.
  4. Prague – a historic city with unique architecture and an unforgettable atmosphere.
  5. Barcelona – a city of Gaudí's buildings, the sea, and energetic urban life.

When choosing a city, it is useful to consider the purpose of the trip. Some people prefer relaxing by the sea, while others enjoy sightseeing stays or a short weekend break. Cities that offer good transport connections, a safe environment, and plenty of cultural activities are considered especially attractive today.

Travel Advice

What to do before travelling

  • check the validity of your documents,
  • compare transport and accommodation prices,
  • prepare a basic itinerary,
  • verify local rules and opening hours of landmarks,
  • have a financial budget ready.

How to travel more efficiently

During a longer trip, it is recommended to reserve important parts of the journey in advance. Especially during the high season, prices are usually higher and capacities fill up quickly. The most practical way of moving between large cities is often by train or plane.

Recommended planning process

  1. choose a country or route,
  2. set the travel dates,
  3. search for transport and accommodation,
  4. make a list of places you want to see,
  5. prepare your budget and important contacts.

Interesting Facts about Europe

  • Europe is home to many countries with different cultural and linguistic traditions.
  • Many European cities are included on the UNESCO World Heritage list.
  • Thanks to its dense railway network, it is possible to travel comfortably even without a car.
Why is Europe attractive for tourists?

Europe offers short distances between countries, a large number of historic landmarks, developed services, and rich cultural events throughout the year.
